Transparent photovoltaics for sustainable energy

Near InfraRed Photon management for transparent LUminescent Spectral conversion technologies

NIR+ targets a new solution for harvesting and conversion of near-infrared photons in a single, transparent, efficient, and modular device for autonomous luminescent light-to-electricity production.

About the project

In the context of sustainable and autonomous energy production and supply, visibly transparent photovoltaics offer major opportunities for seamless integration into architectural elements such as facades and windows. These systems can harvest sunlight while preserving aesthetics, making them highly attractive for the next generation of low-energy and net-zero buildings.

NIR+ addresses the scientific and technological barriers that still limit the competitiveness of transparent photovoltaics. These include the need to balance visible transparency with measurable device efficiency, to reduce complexity for future scale-up and manufacturability, and to optimize photon management under realistic urban illumination conditions where light can be reflected, diffused, and highly variable in angle.

The project adopts a multi-level systemic approach to develop efficient luminescent spectral conversion devices operating in the near-infrared region of the electromagnetic spectrum. Its activities are organized around three scientific and technological pillars, with clearly identified objectives, expected results, and performance targets.

Project objective Develop a transparent and modular NIR-active device for luminescent light-to-electricity conversion.
Application area Architecturally integrated transparent photovoltaics for buildings and urban environments.
Scientific scope Chemistry, materials science, optics, physics, and device engineering in one integrated platform.

Scientific and technological pillars

1

Materials design, development and engineering

NIR+ develops new NIR-active aggregation-induced emitters and integrates them into tailored polymer-based optical lightguides for efficient photon harvesting, luminescence trapping, and light transport.

2

Materials modeling and advanced characterization

Advanced ab-initio and multi-level computational protocols guide material development and help describe the structural, electronic, and optical properties of the new NIR-active systems.

3

Device integration, testing and validation

Photon propagation, confinement, and distribution are optimized through optical modeling to support the proof-of-concept demonstration of visibly transparent lab-scale devices for efficient NIR electricity production.

Why NIR+ matters

The project advances knowledge across multiple disciplines, from chemistry and materials science to optics and device engineering, while supporting the development of practical transparent energy technologies for the built environment.

Its long-term vision is to enable modular, scalable, and efficient transparent luminescent devices capable of operating effectively under realistic urban lighting conditions.

Key goals

  • Preserve visible transparency while improving device performance.
  • Minimize complexity for easier manufacturability and future scale-up.
  • Improve photon management under diffused and variable-angle light.
  • Demonstrate integrated NIR-active transparent luminescent devices.
